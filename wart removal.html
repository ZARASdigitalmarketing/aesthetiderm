Wart Removal

What are Warts?


Warts are small, non-cancerous growths on the skin caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV). They can occur anywhere on the body and appear as raised, rough, and often bumpy patches of skin. Warts can be skin-colored or brownish, and some types of warts may have tiny black dots, which are small blood vessels. Warts can spread from person to person or from one part of the body to another. Moreover, there are several types of warts, including common, plantar, flat, and genital warts.


Types of Warts


There are several different types of warts, each with its distinct appearance and location:
1.Common warts: Usually grow on the fingers, hands, and elbows. Moreover, common warts are raised and rough with a rough surface.
2.Plantar warts: Plantar warts are flat and often painful when applying pressure. Commonly, they develop on the soles of the feet.
3.Flat warts: Smaller and smoother than other types, flat warts are typically develop on the face, neck, legs, and arms.
3. Genital warts: Present in the genital area, genital warts are fleshy and soft growths that spreads through sexual contact.
4. Filiform warts: Long and slender filiform warts are usually develop on the face and neck.
5. Periungual warts: Found around the nails, periungual warts are raise growths that can affect nail growth.


How does it Spreads?


Warts are caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV) and can be spread through direct contact with the virus. This can occur when the virus comes into contact with cuts or broken skin, such as when someone touches a wart on another person's skin or shares personal items that have come into contact with the virus, such as towels or razors. Warts can also spread from one body part to another, especially if someone scratches or picks at the wart.


Signs and Symptoms of Warts


Warts typically appear as small, raised growths on the skin that are usually skin-colored or brown. Some common signs and symptoms of warts include i.e.
1. Rough, bumpy texture: Warts often have a rough, irregular surface that can feel rough to the touch.
2. Small black dots: Some warts may have tiny black dots, which are small blood vessels.
3. Elevated growth: Warts raise above the surrounding skin and may appear as a solid bump or a cluster of bumps.
3. Location-specific appearance: Different types of warts may have distinct appearances. Such as plantar warts that are flat and often painful or genital warts that are fleshy and soft.

How do dermatologists diagnose warts?


A dermatologist can tell whether you have a wart by looking at it. In rare cases, a dermatologist may need to perform a skin biopsy to be certain. If a dermatologist needs to perform a biopsy, the doctor will remove the wart and send it to a lab. At the lab, a small piece of the wart will be looked at under a microscope.
A biopsy is a safe and quick procedure for a dermatologist to perform. It should not cause any anxiety.
