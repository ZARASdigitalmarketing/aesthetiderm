Skin problems


Skin disorders vary greatly in symptoms and severity. They can be temporary or permanent, and may be painless or painful. Some have situational causes, while others may be genetic. Some skin conditions are minor, and others can be life-threatening.

Nail Problems


Common causes of fingernail problems include injury, infection and skin diseases such as eczema and psoriasis. Causes of toenail problems include trauma, ill-fitting shoes, poor circulation, poor nerve supply and infection. Problems with toenails can be successfully treated by a podiatrist.

Some of the causes of nail disorders include the following:
1. Infections (such as paronychia, warts, and green nail syndrome)Injuries.
2. Internal diseases (such as certain lung diseases, which can cause yellow nail syndrome)
