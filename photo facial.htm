Photo Facial

What Is A Photofacial?


Let’s begin by understanding what a photofacial is. This is a unique skin treatment that involves the use of various light-emitting devices to help with skin discoloration while reducing the appearance of blemishes. This is perfect for treating different skin problems like large pores, fine lines, wrinkles, and even acne scars. There are two types of photofacials, i.e., intense pulsed light (IPL) photofacials and light-emitting diode (LED) photo facials. The process takes about half an hour to three hours, depending on the type of photofacial you choose. You might experience some swelling and redness post-procedure, but this will subside within a day or so. The results are gradual, and improvements will appear in a few days following the treatment.


How Do Photofacials Work?


The IPL photofacial involves light being absorbed into your skin. This IPL light is intense, which allows it to be absorbed more deeply by the melanin (pigmentation) and hemoglobin (red blood cells) in the skin. Once the melanin is broken up, the skin absorbs it, which reduces its appearance. The damaged hemoglobin works by stimulating blood flow which causes more efficient melanin absorption.


What Is Getting a Photofacial Like?


IPL photofacials tend to be more complex as compared to LED ones. The procedure begins with an experienced professional applying a cool gel to your face and giving you dark glasses to protect your eyes from the light. Once all the preparation is done, the professionals use a hand-held device that pulses light along your skin. Lastly, you’ll be given a moisturizer and a cool pack to prevent any swelling.


How Long Does the Process Take


IPL photofacials involve a series of treatments, each of which lasts for about 20 minutes to an hour. Typically, clients get 3 to 6 treatments that are spaced one month apart.

How Can You Prepare for the Treatment?


One of the first things to keep in mind when getting an IPL photofacial is that you need to be tan-free. Why? With a tan, the machine won’t be able to accurately detect the specific areas and concerns that you want to be addressed, increasing the risk of being burned. You should avoid alcohol, aspirin, anticoagulants, ibuprofen, or anything that thins the blood.


What Are The Benefits Of Photofacials?


1. REDUCES HYPERPIGMENTATION AND SUN SPOTS

Although IPL Photofacials aren’t an anti-aging solution, these treatments can help in reducing various signs of aging, such as wrinkles, age spots, sunspots, and others. Since this targets the deeper layers of the skin, IPL works by stimulating collagen production and improving the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. It also enhances your skin’s overall texture.


2. REDUCES SKIN REDNESS

If you have rosacea or any other skin condition that diffuses redness into the skin, then IPL therapy is an excellent solution. This treatment reduces the appearance of redness and targets specific areas to create a natural and even-tone complexion.


3. HELPS CONTROL BREAKOUTS

Acne and breakouts can lower one’s self-esteem, making them more conscious about their appearance. IPL treatments can help those with severe acne and who experience redness and inflammation that’s caused by acne. IPL also eliminates large pores and reduces the appearance of acne without the need for topical products that dry out the skin.


4. CAN BE SAFELY COMBINED WITH OTHER PROCEDURES


The best part about IPL treatments is that this can be done along with other skin treatments, such as microdermabrasion, chemical peels, and others that target the outermost layer of your skin. IPL photofacials target the deepest layers of your skin, so choosing this along with treatments that target the outermost layers of skin offer even better results.
