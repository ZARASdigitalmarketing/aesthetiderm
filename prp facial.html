PrP Face

What is PRP Therapy?


PRP therapy is a unique treatment that harnesses the power of platelets, which are well-known for their ability to stimulate cell reproduction, tissue regeneration, and collagen production. During a PRP therapy treatment, one of our experienced staff members will draw your blood and place it in a machine called a centrifuge. This machine rapidly spins the blood to separate the platelets from other components, leaving behind platelet-rich plasma, or what we like to call “liquid gold.” Once the platelet-rich plasma is separated from the blood, your physician will then use this for injections or as a topical treatment.


The Biggest Benefits of PRP


1. Minimized Wrinkles and Lines


One of the most popular reasons people seek out a vampire facial is to reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles. The skin’s life cycle includes the renewal of skin cells, elastin, and collagen. Facial skin tightens spontaneously in reaction to the micro-injuries created by therapy, contributing to the reduction of fine lines and wrinkles. As sagging skin on your face is filled in, the appearance of more severe lines and wrinkles is reduced as well.


2. Firmer and Tighter Skin


Collagen is also necessary for taut, firm skin. Skin sags with age, although not everyone gets wrinkles or creases. Age can cause the skin on your face to thin out, making your lips and cheeks look particularly wrinkled. It’s possible to add volume to your skin using collagen. Some people try it out to change the shape of their cheeks without going under the knife or as an alternative to a CO2 laser. Don’t be afraid to inquire about the specific ways in which a vampire facial might help you reach your beauty goals, no matter how insecure you may be about your skin.


Better Moisture Retention


Having thicker, more robust skin helps keep the inner skin layers safe from environmental aggressors. Additionally, it aids in the removal of built-up dead skin cells, facilitating the absorption of topical ointments. It’s unclear how a vampire facial affects pores, especially since therapy might minimize pores’ visibility. On the other hand, having your pores minimized and unclogged can help you better absorb beauty-enhancing prescription products.

Long-Lasting Results


One of the primary benefits of PRP therapy is that the results are long-lasting. In fact, for most people, the results of microneedling with PRP therapy aren’t fully visible until a couple of months after the treatment session. This is because it takes some time for the body’s natural healing process to respond and reveal treatment results.


PRP Facial is Painless


The first step of a PRP facial is thoroughly cleansing the skin. That way, you won’t have to worry about any potential side effects from dirt, pollen, pollutants, cosmetics, or any other impurities on your skin. Your dermatologist will use a local anesthetic after they have cleaned your face. Even though you’ll be able to sense the treatment being administered, it won’t hurt or feel uncomfortable. Aspirin can be used either before or after therapy to lessen the likelihood of discomfort. Traditional microneedling often results in noticeable redness for up to seven days. When combined with platelet-rich plasma, you can avoid this recovery period. Injecting platelet-rich plasma helps cure the micro-injuries caused by microneedling almost instantly.


PRP Results Are Long-Lasting


Some anti-aging therapies need to be repeated after just a week or two at most. However, a vampire facial is all you need about once a year to keep your youthful appearance intact. Better still, a PRP facial requires fewer sessions than conventional microneedling to obtain the desired results.
Microneedling treatments typically last 30 minutes, but they can take up to an hour if you have to wait for the anesthesia to take effect afterward. Vampire facials, on the other hand, only require half of it.
Most people who want younger-looking skin will need four treatments, with each session separated out by four to eight weeks. When applied to keloid scars, microneedling combined with PRP can significantly improve their look after just five treatments. Two to four treatments are needed to remove acne scars.
